    Section 2 2022-02-22 Large Fields

    A field \(k\) is called large if no smooth curves \(C/k\) have nonempty finite \(C(k)\text{.}\) Equivalently, if \(k\) is large, then a smooth curve \(C/k\) has either empty \(C(k)\) or infinite \(C(k)\text{.}\) The notion is due to F. Pop. In Bary-Soroker and Fehm, Question I asks whether \(\mathbb Q(\mu_\infty)\text{,}\) the field generated by all roots of unity, is large or not.
    Let \(X(N)\) be the modular curve for the full level-\(N\) structure, where \(N\ge1\) is an integer. Since its cusps are defined over \(\mathbb Q(\mu_N)\text{,}\) \(X(N)(\mathbb Q(\mu_\infty))\) is non-empty. If \(\mathbb Q(\mu_\infty)\) were large, then \(X(N)(\mathbb Q(\mu_{\infty}))\) must be infinite.
    1. Can we prove whether \(X(N)(\mathbb Q(\mu_\infty))\) is infinite for all \(N\text{?}\)
    2. Can we prove whether \(X(N)(\mathbb Q(\mu_\infty))\) is infinite for infinitely many \(N\text{?}\)
    We can ask similar questions for \(X_0(N)\) or \(X_1(N)\text{.}\)
    Here are some motivations for the questions above. If \(X(N)(\mathbb Q(\mu_\infty))\) is infinite, it means that there is a sequence of elliptic curves \(E_i/\mathbb Q(\mu_{M_i})\text{,}\) \(i=1,2,\cdots\text{,}\) such that \(E_i[N]\) generates an absolutely abelian field. Then, for \(p \mid N\text{,}\) \(E_i[p^\infty]\) would generated a pro-\(p\) extension, say \(F_\infty\) of an abelian field \(F\text{.}\) To the extension \(F_\infty/F\text{,}\) the theorem of Coates—Sujatha can be applied, verifying some instance of the \(\mathfrak M_H(G)\)-conjecture.
